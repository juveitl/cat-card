Cat

The cat (Felis catus) is a small carnivorous mammal. It is the only domesticated species in the family Felidae and often referred to as the domestic cat to distinguish it from wild members of the family.

  • Size: It averages about 46 cm (18 in) in head-to-body length.
  • Vision: Cats have excellent night vision and can see at only one-sixth the light level required for human vision.
  • Hearing: The domestic cat's hearing is most acute in the range of 500 Hz to 32 kHz.
  • Taste: Cats have relatively few taste buds compared to humans (470 or so versus more than 9,000 on the human tongue).

As of 2017, the domestic cat was the second-most popular pet in the United States by number of pets owned, after freshwater fish.
